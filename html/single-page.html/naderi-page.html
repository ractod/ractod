Naderi

Firooz Naderi was born on April 25, 1958 in Shiraz. He completed his secondary education in Iran and then immigrated to the United States, where he pursued a university degree in electrical engineering. He completed his bachelor's degree at Iowa State University and his master's and doctoral degrees in 1972 and 1976 at the University of Southern California. Naderi returned to Iran after graduation and while serving in the army, he worked for some time in the Remote Sensing Center of Iran. During his time with NASA, he held a number of technical and managerial positions in the field of mobile communications satellites, remote sensing radars, astrophysics observatories, Mars exploration, and other solar system objects. . He joined JPL in 1976 as director of space science flight experiments and director of NASA's Scatterometer project. In addition, he oversaw the Advanced Satellite Communications Technology Program at NASA and the Mobile Satellite Program Manager at JPL. Since 2000, he has been managing the newly established Mars Plan. The mission of the Mars Program Center is to direct and plan all research related to Mars. In April 2000, he was appointed Director of Mars Exploration Projects. Following the success of Mars probes, Naderi was appointed Deputy Director and Senior Planning Director of JPL (Jet Propulsion Laboratory), one of the most important space centers in NASA, and in his new position as Head Designing the Center's plans and strategies, it has used its experience on Mars missions to study other parts of the world from Earth to distant galaxies. He is also responsible for designing JPL's five- to 20-year strategic vision. In 2011, he was appointed director of robotic research for the solar system.
